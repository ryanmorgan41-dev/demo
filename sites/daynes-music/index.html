1862 to today

A century and a half of music, kept in one family.

Founded the same decade Utah was still a territory, Daynes has been a fixture of Salt Lake's musical life ever since. Little has changed about the idea: find people the right instrument, then teach them to use it.

The floor

A room you can hear before you see it

Pianos are meant to be played before they are bought. The showroom keeps a standing floor of grands and uprights so you can sit, listen, and feel the difference between one instrument and the next before you ever decide.

The studio

Where an instrument becomes a habit

Selling a piano is half the job. The lesson program is the other half: the reason so many Salt Lake families learned to play under the same name their grandparents did. Teachers meet students where they are, from a first scale to a hard concerto.

Beyond the keys

More than pianos under the roof

The name is built on pianos, but the walls carry more: guitars, strings, band instruments and everything that goes with them. Whatever a household wants to learn, the goal is the same one it has been since 1862, to put the right instrument in the right hands.
